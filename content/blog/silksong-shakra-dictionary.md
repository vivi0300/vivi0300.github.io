# Shakra Dictionary

## Remarks

Everything she says is either an exclamation / noun (e.g. Hello) or idiom (e.g. What the Hell). The phonemes are very klingon-esque in general. Notably, in her most lengthy and sincere dialogue (trail's end and act 3), she barely speaks any of her mother tongue, but she says a lot of new words after fighting with Hornet.

## Definitions
##### Poshanka
a message that says you hope someone is well, happy, etc.:

- *"**Poshanka**! Edges sharp and senses keen!"*

##### Gara Takana
An expression of usually slight anger or surprise, or a way of adding force to a statement, question, etc.

- *"**Gara Takana**! What has happened to you, Child Wielding Needle"*
##### Yakkanesh
Used for telling someone to give attention to what they are doing because there is a risk they could have an accident, make a mistake, or damage something:

- ***Yakkanesh**! Keep your weapon drawn and your bearings true!*
##### Ka, va
an expression of disgust (= disapproval and dislike):

- ***Ka, va**! Your shell is ridden with those gruesome maggots.*
##### Okklu-din
used to show that a question is difficult to answer or that the answer is not immediately clear:

- ***Okklu-Din**... a perceptive question. I travel in search of my mentor, the greatest of my tribe.*

##### Dondakku
to call out to express disapproval or strong disagreement:

- ***Dondakku**! Where are the armaments? The watch-poles? Never have I seen such a poorly defended encampment!*
##### Yokkala
To indicate that you understand what someone is telling you

- ***Yokkala**... Perhaps so. They must have prized indeed the work done here (...)*
##### Nu-Hakkata
an idea, something said or written, or behaviour that is silly or stupid: 

- ***Nu-Hakkata**! Whether she is dead or alive, have faith!*
##### Hakk
used as an informal greeting between people who know each other or as an expression of approval: 

- *Needle Child. Hakk! I encountered a settlement near here (...)*
##### Garrok
used for telling people that it is time to start moving or doing something with energy and enthusiasm:

- *Garrok! Hornet Wielding Needle. Beside you, I will gladly raise my rings!*
##### Bakkana
used to emphasize that you strongly agree or disagree, like or dislike something, etc.:

- *Bakkana! A battle well won!*
##### Un-daak
used for praising someone for something they have done well: 

- *Un-Daak... well struck. To face the fury of your weapon in a true battle would be an experience indeed.*
##### Bakelo
Yes, certainly:

- *Bakelo! Answer to your tasks, then. My rings shall await your return.*
##### Gendaa
Something you say when you are happy because something bad did not happen:

- ***Gendaa**... This is good, Hornet Wielding Needle, essential.*
